Andaman and Nicobar Island

The name Andaman might have been derived from Handuman, after the Indian God Hanuman from the Hindu epic Ramayana.[5] The place was called with a similar name by the Malays, who used to be involved in slave trade in the region.[6] The place was also referred by various names such as Angademan by Ptolemy in the 2nd century CE and Angamanian by Marco Polo in 13th century CE.[5] Nicobar, which was located in the sea route connecting South India to South East Asia, was known as Nakkavaram, meaning "open/naked land" borrowed from Tamil language which later became Nicobar.[7] In the middle ages (500-1500 AD), Nicobar was known as Lankhabatus in Arabia, probably a mis-transcription of the name Nakkavaram.

  1. Radhanagar Beach – One of Asia’s best beaches located on Havelock Island.
  2. Cellular Jail – A historic colonial prison with a poignant light and sound show.
  3. Ross Island – Former British administrative headquarters with haunting ruins.
  4. Baratang Island – Famous for its limestone caves and mangrove boat rides.
  5. Neil Island – Known for tranquil beaches like Bharatpur and Laxmanpur.
  • Fish Curry – A flavorful local delicacy made with freshly caught fish and spices.
  • Grilled Lobster – A seafood lover’s delight, served fresh and juicy.
  • Coconut Prawn Curry – A creamy, aromatic dish made with local coconuts and prawns.
  • Amritsari Kulcha – A North Indian favorite popular among locals and tourists alike.
  • Banana Chips – A crispy snack often served on the islands, made from local bananas.
